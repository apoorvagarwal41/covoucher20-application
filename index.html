We help local businesses through the pandemic

CoVoucher20 is a platform for consumers to buy vouchers, use them later, and support local businesses. Be there for them today, so they can be there for you tomorrow.

A word from the founder

Miguel Morin

Hello!

My name is Miguel Morin and I founded CoVoucher20. I studied the Great Depression of 1929 for my PhD in Economics. In the US during the 1930s, millions of people lost their businesses and their homes; they lived in makeshift camps called Hoovervilles.

When countries started locking down, I knew that a crisis of this size could mean death for millions of local businesses. So I used my background in software development and my recent passion in entrepreneurship to launch CoVoucher20 in record time.

Before CoVoucher20, I had studied computer science at Ecole Polytechnique in Paris; done a PhD in Economics at Columbia University in New York; worked at the Faculty of Economics at the University of Cambridge in the UK; worked in artificial intelligence at The Alan Turing Institute in London; and launched Puzzli, a smart toy to teach children to read.

The challenge

Everyone is talking about it: local business owners face an existential threat. Local business owners may be unable to pay staff, utility bills, and even their mortgages. Owners and employees need those homes to stay safe from the virus.

We believe that generosity is the answer: consumers can support valued business that they want use after the crisis. The finances of the business may take a dip but they won't completely crash.

We can avoid a crash in our local communities by keeping the money flowing and helping businesses adapt and survive.

How it works

  • 1 Local businesses sign up, enter their location, explain their business, and enter details.
  • 2 Businesses share a direct link to their CoVoucher20 page with their customer base.
  • 3 Patrons sign up and buy vouchers that they would like to use after the pandemic.
  • 4 Businesses receive money immediately and are better able to cope.
